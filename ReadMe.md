# Business Questions Addressed

-- What 3 to 5 KPIs should executives monitor?

Total customers contacted
This shows the overall size of the marketing campaign and gives executives context for the scale of outreach.

Total subscribers
This shows how many customers accepted the term deposit offer.

Conversion rate
This is the most important KPI because it shows how effective the campaign was at turning contacted customers into subscribers.

Average contact count
This helps show how much effort was required to reach customers.

Previous success conversion rate
This shows how customers who had a successful previous campaign outcome performed.


-- Which customer segment would you recommend targeting first and why?

I would recommend targeting customers who previously had a successful campaign outcome, have no housing loan, no personal loan, and a balance above $500.
This segment should be targeted first because they are much more likely to subscribe compared with the overall customer base. They also have characteristics that the bank can identify before starting the campaign, making the segment practical for future targeting.
These customers already had a positive experience with the bank and have sufficient account balances to make a term deposit offer.

-- Based on your dashboard, what top actions should the bank take to improve the next marketing campaign?

I would prioritize customers with a prior successful campaign outcome as the strongest opportunity. The bank should place these customers at the top of the next campaign list.
Secondly, I would then focus on customers with fewer loan obligations. Customers without housing or personal loans show higher conversion rates. 
Lastly, the data show that conversion is strongest at lower contact counts and declines as customers are contacted more times. This could suggest that the bank should refine the initial target selection group before the campaign starts.
